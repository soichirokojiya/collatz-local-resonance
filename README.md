# Local Resonance in Block-Compressed Collatz Dynamics

*A Status-Aware Working Paper on Local Resonance in Block-Compressed Collatz Dynamics*

**Author:** Soichiro Kojiya, Independent Researcher  
**Date:** April 2026  
**Status:** Working Paper (near-complete proof architecture)

## Abstract

We organize a branch-driven affine skew-product approach to block-compressed odd-to-odd Collatz dynamics, with emphasis on local resonance, deterministic tails, frozen stages, recurrence, and rank-depletion of independent obstructions. All major global branches are reduced to frozen, almost-neutral periodic, or structural-degeneration regimes, and the remaining burden is sharply localized in a small local normal-form package for same-stage cells together with its final one-sided admissibility consequences.

## Structure

1. Block-compressed odd-to-odd dynamics
2. Candidate restriction and threshold-near geometry
3. Semiconvergents and same-stage sparse counting
4. Width control, spacing, and deterministic tails
5. Fixed-stage blocks: separation, freezing, recurrence, degeneration
6. Global supply of independent blocks and rank depletion
7. Stage-transition rigidity and absence of generic persistent local resonance
8. The local normal-form package
